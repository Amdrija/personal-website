I had learned to code when I was 11 years old, in Turbo Pascal (yes, the blue screen IDE without syntax highlighting, good times). Right now, you are probably thinking that I was a child prodigy, destined to be a 1337x programmer, but I didn’t really see myself that way. Back then, I hated going to school, just as most of the kids did. I looked for every opportunity not to attend classes. In my elementary school, if you went to competitions, you could miss classes in order to “prepare” for the competitions. Naturally, I went to as many such competitions as I could. Therefore, I would occasionally miss classes to prepare, while, in reality, I would just stay home and play League of Legends. Programming came in handy, as I seemed to be good at it, so my professor started preparing me to attend competitions.
